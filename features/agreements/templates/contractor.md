**Effective Date:** January 10, 2026

## 1. Agreement Overview

This Independent Contractor Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between TSmart Warehouse ("Company") and [Contractor Name] ("Contractor"). This Agreement governs the provision of services by Contractor to Company.

## 2. Independent Contractor Status

2.1. **Relationship**: Contractor is an independent contractor, NOT an employee, agent, partner, or joint venture of Company.

2.2. **Independence**: Contractor:
- Has control over how services are performed
- Provides own tools and equipment
- Sets own work schedule (subject to deadlines)
- May work for other clients
- Is not entitled to employee benefits

2.3. **Consequences**: As independent contractor:
- No employment benefits (health insurance, paid leave, etc.)
- Responsible for own taxes and social security
- Not covered by Company's workers' compensation
- No unemployment benefits
- Must maintain own business insurance

## 6. Work Product and Intellectual Property

6.1. **Work Product**: All work product created under this Agreement is "Work for Hire" and belongs to Company:
- Code, designs, documents, content
- Ideas, concepts, inventions
- Anything created in performance of services

6.2. **Assignment**: Contractor assigns all rights, title, and interest in work product to Company.

6.3. **Moral Rights**: Contractor waives moral rights to extent permitted by law.

6.4. **Pre-Existing IP**: Contractor may use pre-existing IP (e.g., code libraries, templates) with Company's knowledge:
- Must be disclosed in advance
- Contractor grants Company license to use in work product
- Pre-existing IP remains Contractor's property

6.5. **License Grant**: For any pre-existing IP incorporated in work product:
- Perpetual, worldwide, royalty-free license to Company
- Non-exclusive or exclusive (specify: [Non-exclusive])

6.6. **Third-Party IP**: Contractor will not incorporate third-party IP without:
- Proper licensing
- Company's prior approval
- Warranty of right to use

6.7. **Portfolio**: Contractor may use work product in portfolio:
- **After**: Project completion and launch (or [X days] after completion)
- **Limitations**: May not disclose confidential information
- **Attribution**: May credit self in portfolio

## 7. Confidentiality

7.1. **Confidential Information**: Contractor agrees to protect:
- Business information (customers, strategies, financials)
- Technical information (code, designs, specs)
- Product information (roadmap, unreleased features)
- Any information marked confidential or reasonably understood as confidential

7.2. **Obligations**:
- Not disclose to third parties
- Not use for Contractor's benefit or others' benefit
- Protect with same care as Contractor's own confidential information (minimum: reasonable care)

7.3. **Exceptions**: Information that is:
- Public domain
- Already known to Contractor
- Independently developed
- Required by law to disclose (with notice to Company)

7.4. **Duration**: 5 years after termination of Agreement.

7.5. **Return**: Upon termination, return or destroy all confidential materials.

## 8. Representations and Warranties

8.1. **Contractor Warrants**:
- Authority to enter this Agreement
- Services will be performed professionally
- Work product is original and doesn't infringe IP rights
- Has necessary skills and qualifications
- Complies with all applicable laws
- No conflicts of interest

8.2. **No Conflicts**: Contractor is not restricted by any agreement that would prevent performance under this Agreement.

8.3. **Warranty Period**: 90 days after delivery for defects or non-conformance.

8.4. **Remedy**: Company will notify Contractor of issues; Contractor will correct promptly at no additional charge.

## 9. Indemnification

9.1. **Contractor Indemnifies Company**: Against claims arising from:
- Infringement of IP rights due to Contractor's work
- Contractor's breach of Agreement
- Contractor's negligence or misconduct
- Violation of laws by Contractor

9.2. **Company Indemnifies Contractor**: Against claims arising from:
- Company's use of work product as authorized
- Company's breach of Agreement
- Claims related to Company's business (not related to services)

9.3. **Process**: 
- Indemnified party notifies indemnifying party promptly
- Indemnifying party controls defense
- Indemnified party cooperates

## 10. Limitation of Liability

10.1. **Cap**: Each party's liability limited to:
- Total fees paid (or to be paid) under this Agreement
- Or [specific amount] TRY (whichever is greater)

10.2. **Excluded Damages**: Neither party liable for:
- Indirect, incidental, consequential damages
- Lost profits, revenue, or business opportunities
- Cost of substitute services

10.3. **Exceptions**: Limitation doesn't apply to:
- IP infringement claims
- Breach of confidentiality
- Fraud or willful misconduct
- Personal injury or death

10.4. **Reasonable Allocation**: Parties acknowledge this allocation of risk is reasonable.

11.3. **Public Announcements**: Neither party will announce relationship without other's approval (except factual statements like "X worked with Y on Z project").

13.3. **Reasonableness**: Restrictions are reasonable to protect Company's business interests.

- Must have legal right to work in Turkey
- Responsible for obtaining necessary visas/permits

## 16. Dispute Resolution

16.1. **Negotiation**: Parties will first attempt to resolve disputes through good-faith negotiation.

16.2. **Mediation**: If unresolved, parties agree to mediation before legal action.

16.3. **Arbitration** (optional):
- Binding arbitration under [Istanbul Arbitration Centre] rules
- Single arbitrator
- Istanbul, Turkey
- Turkish law applies

16.4. **Litigation**: If arbitration not elected, disputes resolved in Istanbul courts under Turkish law.

16.5. **Attorneys' Fees**: Prevailing party may recover reasonable attorneys' fees.

## 17. General Provisions

17.1. **Entire Agreement**: This Agreement is the complete agreement, superseding all prior agreements.

17.2. **Amendments**: Must be in writing and signed by both parties (email with clear acceptance is acceptable).

17.3. **Governing Law**: Turkish law governs this Agreement.

17.4. **Severability**: Invalid provisions don't affect remaining provisions.

17.5. **Waiver**: Failure to enforce doesn't waive right to enforce later.

17.6. **Assignment**:
- Contractor may not assign without Company's written consent
- Company may assign to affiliates or in connection with merger/acquisition

17.7. **Force Majeure**: Neither party liable for delays due to events beyond reasonable control (natural disasters, wars, pandemics, etc.).

17.8. **Notices**: 
- In writing
- Email acceptable to: [Company email] and [Contractor email]
- Effective upon receipt

17.9. **Counterparts**: Agreement may be executed in counterparts (including electronic).

17.10. **Language**: If translated, Turkish version controls in case of conflict.
