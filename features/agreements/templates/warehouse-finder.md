# Warehouse Finder Agreement

**Effective Date:** January 10, 2026  
**Version:** 1.0

## 1. Agreement Overview

This Warehouse Finder Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between TSmart Warehouse ("Company") and the Warehouse Finder ("Finder"). This Agreement governs the terms under which Finder may discover, evaluate, and recommend warehouse properties for inclusion in Company's platform.

## 2. Appointment as Warehouse Finder

2.1. **Role Definition**: Company appoints Finder as an independent contractor to identify and evaluate potential warehouse properties suitable for the Company's platform.

2.2. **Territory**: Finder's operational area is: [To be specified upon agreement acceptance]

2.3. **Term**: This Agreement is effective from the date of acceptance and continues for an initial term of 12 months, automatically renewable.

## 3. Finder Responsibilities

3.1. **Property Discovery**: Finder agrees to:
- Identify potential warehouse properties within their territory
- Research property ownership and contact information
- Conduct preliminary property assessments
- Document findings in the Company's CRM system
- Maintain accurate records of all discovery activities

3.2. **Property Evaluation**: Finder will:
- Visit properties and conduct on-site inspections
- Photograph and document property features
- Assess property condition and suitability
- Collect technical specifications (size, capacity, accessibility)
- Evaluate location advantages and logistics factors
- Identify any concerns or limitations

3.3. **Owner Engagement**: Finder will:
- Initiate contact with property owners
- Present Company's platform and value proposition
- Schedule follow-up meetings and property tours
- Facilitate introductions between owners and Company
- Maintain professional communication standards

3.4. **Documentation**: All activities must be logged in the CRM system including:
- Property details and specifications
- Contact information and communication history
- Visit reports and assessment findings
- Photos and supporting documentation
- Owner interest level and timeline

## 4. Compensation Structure

4.1. **Discovery Fee**: 
- Initial property discovery and logging: $50 per qualified property
- Qualified property criteria: Minimum 5,000 sq ft, suitable for commercial storage

4.2. **Visit Fee**:
- On-site property inspection and report: $200 per completed visit
- Includes: comprehensive inspection, photo documentation, technical report

4.3. **Success Fee**: When a discovered property is onboarded to the platform:
- One-time success fee: $1,000 upon warehouse owner completing registration
- Monthly recurring commission: 5% of warehouse owner's revenue for first 12 months
- Renewal commission: 3% of revenue for subsequent 12 months

4.4. **Bonus Structure**:
- 5+ properties onboarded in a quarter: 10% bonus on success fees
- 10+ properties onboarded in a quarter: 20% bonus on success fees
- Premium properties (>50,000 sq ft): Additional $500 bonus

4.5. **Payment Terms**:
- Discovery and visit fees: Paid monthly within 30 days
- Success fees: Paid upon warehouse owner's first booking payment
- Monthly commissions: Paid 30 days after month end
- Minimum payout threshold: $100

## 5. Quality Standards

5.1. **Property Criteria**: Properties must meet minimum standards:
- Appropriate zoning and permits for commercial storage
- Safe and secure environment
- Reasonable accessibility for customers
- Adequate infrastructure (power, loading facilities)
- Compliant with local regulations

5.2. **Documentation Quality**: All submissions must include:
- Clear, well-lit photographs (minimum 10 photos)
- Accurate measurements and specifications
- Complete owner contact information
- Assessment of property condition
- Notes on special features or limitations

5.3. **Verification**: Company reserves the right to verify all information. Inaccurate or misleading information may result in fee forfeiture.

## 6. Company Responsibilities

6.1. **Training and Support**: Company will provide:
- Onboarding and training materials
- CRM system access and training
- Property evaluation guidelines and checklists
- Marketing materials about the platform
- Technical support for system questions

6.2. **Lead Assignment**: Company may assign specific properties or areas for Finder to investigate.

6.3. **Owner Engagement**: Company will:
- Manage final negotiations with property owners
- Handle all legal and contractual matters
- Process warehouse owner registrations
- Provide ongoing owner support

## 7. Exclusivity and Territory

7.1. **Non-Exclusive**: This is a non-exclusive agreement. Company may engage multiple Finders in overlapping territories.

7.2. **Property Registration**: First Finder to properly register a property in the CRM system receives credit for that property.

7.3. **Protected Period**: Once registered, property is protected for Finder for 90 days. If owner doesn't onboard within 90 days, property may be reassigned.

## 8. Conduct and Ethics

8.1. **Professional Standards**: Finder must:
- Conduct all activities professionally and ethically
- Respect property owners' time and privacy
- Accurately represent Company and its services
- Maintain confidentiality of sensitive information
- Follow all applicable laws and regulations

8.2. **Prohibited Activities**: Finder must not:
- Make unauthorized commitments on Company's behalf
- Offer terms or pricing not approved by Company
- Engage in deceptive or misleading practices
- Trespass on private property
- Harass or pressure property owners
- Collect personal data beyond what's necessary

8.3. **Safety**: Finder is responsible for their own safety during property visits and must follow appropriate safety precautions.

## 9. Confidentiality

9.1. **Confidential Information**: Finder agrees to protect:
- Property owner contact information and details
- Company's business strategies and pricing
- Platform features and roadmap information
- Other Finders' activities and territories
- Customer data and business metrics

9.2. **Use Restriction**: Confidential information may only be used for authorized Finder activities.

9.3. **Post-Termination**: Confidentiality obligations continue for 2 years after termination.

## 10. Independent Contractor Status

10.1. **Relationship**: Finder is an independent contractor, not an employee, agent, or partner of Company.

10.2. **Taxes**: Finder is responsible for all taxes, insurance, and expenses related to their activities.

10.3. **No Benefits**: Finder is not entitled to employee benefits, including health insurance, retirement plans, or paid time off.

10.4. **Expenses**: Finder is responsible for their own business expenses (travel, equipment, etc.) unless otherwise agreed in writing.

## 11. Intellectual Property

11.1. **Work Product**: All reports, assessments, photographs, and other materials created by Finder are Company's property.

11.2. **License to Use**: Company grants Finder a limited license to use Company's trademarks and materials solely for authorized Finder activities.

11.3. **No Transfer**: Finder may not transfer, sell, or license any Company intellectual property to third parties.

## 12. Liability and Insurance

12.1. **Limitation of Liability**: Company's liability is limited to unpaid fees owed to Finder.

12.2. **Indemnification**: Finder agrees to indemnify Company against claims arising from:
- Finder's negligence or misconduct
- Violations of laws or regulations
- Unauthorized representations or actions
- Property damage or personal injury during visits

12.3. **Insurance**: Finder is encouraged to maintain appropriate liability insurance.

## 13. Non-Compete and Non-Solicitation

13.1. **Non-Compete**: During the term and for 12 months after termination, Finder will not:
- Create or join a competing warehouse marketplace platform
- Directly onboard discovered properties to competing platforms
- Use Company's contacts or information for competing purposes

13.2. **Non-Solicitation**: Finder will not solicit Company's employees, contractors, warehouse owners, or customers for 12 months after termination.

## 14. Termination

14.1. **Termination for Cause**: Either party may terminate immediately for:
- Material breach of this Agreement
- Fraud, misrepresentation, or dishonesty
- Violation of laws or regulations
- Damage to Company's reputation
- Safety violations or reckless behavior

14.2. **Termination for Convenience**: Either party may terminate with 30 days written notice.

14.3. **Post-Termination**:
- Pending fees for completed work will be paid
- Finder must return all Company materials and property
- Confidentiality obligations continue
- CRM access will be revoked
- Properties in pipeline (registered within last 90 days) remain credited to Finder if they onboard within 180 days of termination

## 15. Dispute Resolution

15.1. **Negotiation**: Disputes will first be addressed through good-faith negotiation.

15.2. **Mediation**: If unresolved, disputes will be submitted to mediation.

15.3. **Arbitration**: Final disputes will be resolved through binding arbitration.

15.4. **Governing Law**: This Agreement is governed by the laws of [Jurisdiction].

16.1. **Entire Agreement**: This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement and supersedes all prior agreements.

16.2. **Amendments**: Must be in writing and signed by both parties.

16.3. **Assignment**: Finder may not assign this Agreement without Company's written consent.

16.4. **Severability**: Invalid provisions do not affect the validity of remaining provisions.

16.5. **Notice**: All notices must be in writing and sent to the addresses on file.

## 17. Performance Metrics

17.1. **Expected Activity**: While not mandatory, Finder is encouraged to discover at least 5 properties per month to maintain active status.

17.2. **Quality Score**: Company tracks quality metrics including:
- Property qualification rate (% of discoveries that meet criteria)
- Documentation completeness score
- Owner onboarding success rate
- Response time to Company requests

17.3. **Performance Reviews**: Quarterly reviews to assess performance and provide feedback.
