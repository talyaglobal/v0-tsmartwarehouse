# Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)

**Effective Date:** January 10, 2026  
**Version:** 1.0

## 1. Agreement Overview

This Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between TSmart Warehouse ("Disclosing Party") and the recipient ("Receiving Party"). This Agreement protects confidential information shared between the parties.

## 2. Purpose

The parties wish to explore a business relationship and in connection with this exploration, Disclosing Party may share confidential information with Receiving Party. This Agreement protects such information from unauthorized disclosure or use.

## 3. Definition of Confidential Information

3.1. **Confidential Information** includes, but is not limited to:

**Business Information:**
- Business plans, strategies, and forecasts
- Financial information, pricing, and cost structures
- Customer lists, contacts, and relationship information
- Supplier and vendor information
- Marketing plans and strategies
- Revenue, profit, and sales data

**Technical Information:**
- Software source code and algorithms
- Technical specifications and documentation
- Database schemas and structures
- API specifications and integration details
- System architecture and design documents
- Security protocols and measures

**Operational Information:**
- Internal processes and procedures
- Operational metrics and KPIs
- Training materials and documentation
- Employee information
- Contract terms and conditions
- Negotiation positions

**Intellectual Property:**
- Trade secrets and know-how
- Proprietary methodologies
- Unpublished patent applications
- Trademark strategies
- Product roadmaps and development plans

3.2. **Form of Information**: Confidential Information may be in written, electronic, oral, or visual form.

3.3. **Marking**: Written or electronic Confidential Information will typically be marked "Confidential" or with a similar designation. Oral Confidential Information will be identified as confidential at the time of disclosure and confirmed in writing within 30 days.

## 4. Exclusions from Confidential Information

Confidential Information does not include information that:

4.1. **Public Domain**: Is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by Receiving Party

4.2. **Prior Knowledge**: Was rightfully in Receiving Party's possession before disclosure by Disclosing Party

4.3. **Independent Development**: Is independently developed by Receiving Party without use of or reference to Confidential Information

4.4. **Lawful Third Party**: Is rightfully obtained from a third party without restriction and without breach of any obligation to Disclosing Party

4.5. **Approved Release**: Is approved for release by written authorization from Disclosing Party

## 5. Obligations of Receiving Party

5.1. **Non-Disclosure**: Receiving Party agrees to:
- Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence
- Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent
- Use the same degree of care to protect Confidential Information as for their own confidential information (but not less than reasonable care)

5.2. **Permitted Use**: Receiving Party may only use Confidential Information for:
- Evaluating or pursuing the business relationship with Disclosing Party
- Purposes explicitly authorized by Disclosing Party in writing

5.3. **Limited Disclosure**: Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information only to:
- Employees, contractors, or advisors who have a legitimate need to know
- Individuals who have been informed of the confidential nature and agree to be bound by obligations at least as restrictive as this Agreement

5.4. **Notice of Breach**: Receiving Party must:
- Immediately notify Disclosing Party of any unauthorized disclosure or use
- Cooperate in efforts to prevent further unauthorized disclosure
- Take all reasonable steps to mitigate damage

## 6. Required Disclosures

6.1. **Legal Compulsion**: If Receiving Party is legally compelled to disclose Confidential Information:
- Provide prompt written notice to Disclosing Party (if legally permitted)
- Cooperate with Disclosing Party's efforts to seek protective orders
- Disclose only the minimum information required by law
- Request confidential treatment of disclosed information

6.2. **Government Inquiries**: Similar procedures apply to government or regulatory inquiries.

## 7. No License or Ownership

7.1. **No Rights Granted**: This Agreement does not grant Receiving Party any license, rights, or ownership in Confidential Information except the limited right to review for the stated purpose.

7.2. **No Obligation to Disclose**: Disclosing Party is not obligated to disclose any particular information.

7.3. **No Business Relationship**: This Agreement does not create any partnership, joint venture, employment, or agency relationship.

## 8. Return or Destruction of Information

8.1. **Upon Request**: Upon Disclosing Party's written request, Receiving Party must:
- Promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information in any form
- Certify in writing that all Confidential Information has been returned or destroyed
- Permanently delete all electronic copies (except for automatically created backup copies that are destroyed in the normal course)

8.2. **Retention Exception**: Legal or regulatory requirements to retain documents take precedence, but confidentiality obligations continue.

## 9. No Representations or Warranties

9.1. **As-Is**: All Confidential Information is provided "AS-IS" without any representation or warranty of accuracy, completeness, or fitness for purpose.

9.2. **No Liability**: Disclosing Party is not liable for any errors or omissions in Confidential Information.

## 10. Term and Termination

10.1. **Effective Term**: This Agreement is effective from the date of acceptance.

10.2. **Confidentiality Period**: Obligations regarding Confidential Information continue for:
- **Trade Secrets**: As long as the information remains a trade secret under applicable law
- **Other Confidential Information**: Five (5) years from the date of disclosure

10.3. **Termination**: Either party may terminate the business discussions at any time, but confidentiality obligations continue per Section 10.2.

10.4. **Survival**: Sections 3-9 and 11-14 survive termination.
