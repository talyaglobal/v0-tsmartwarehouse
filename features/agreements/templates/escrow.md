# Escrow Agreement

**Effective Date:** January 10, 2026  
**Version:** 1.0

## 1. Agreement Overview

This Escrow Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between TSmart Warehouse ("Platform"), the Customer ("Buyer"), and the Warehouse Owner ("Seller"), with funds held by an approved Escrow Agent. This Agreement protects both parties in high-value warehouse storage transactions.

## 2. Purpose of Escrow

2.1. **Protection**: Escrow services provide protection for:
- High-value storage contracts (typically over 100,000 TRY)
- Long-term storage commitments (12+ months)
- First-time transactions between parties
- International transactions
- Custom warehouse modifications requiring upfront investment

2.2. **Neutral Third Party**: Escrow Agent acts as a neutral intermediary, holding funds until agreed conditions are met.

## 3. Escrow Eligibility

3.1. **Minimum Transaction**: Escrow is typically required or available for transactions exceeding 100,000 TRY.

3.2. **Voluntary Escrow**: Either party may request escrow for smaller transactions, subject to Platform approval and fee considerations.

3.3. **Mandatory Escrow**: Platform may require escrow for:
- New warehouse owners without established track record
- International transactions
- Transactions flagged by fraud prevention systems
- Custom contracts with significant upfront investments

## 4. Approved Escrow Agents

4.1. **Designated Agents**: Platform maintains a list of approved escrow agents:
- Licensed Turkish financial institutions
- International escrow service providers (for cross-border transactions)
- Legal firms with escrow capabilities

4.2. **Agent Selection**: 
- Platform typically designates the escrow agent
- Parties may mutually agree on alternative approved agent
- Agent must be acceptable to all parties

4.3. **Agent Credentials**: All escrow agents must:
- Be licensed and regulated
- Maintain appropriate insurance
- Have established escrow procedures
- Provide transparent fee schedules

## 5. Escrow Process

### 5.1. Initiation Phase

**Step 1: Escrow Agreement**
- All parties review and sign this Escrow Agreement
- Specific transaction details added as Exhibit A
- Escrow Agent confirms acceptance

**Step 2: Escrow Instructions**
- Detailed instructions prepared outlining:
  - Amount to be escrowed
  - Release conditions
  - Timeline and milestones
  - Contingencies and dispute procedures
- All parties acknowledge and agree to instructions

**Step 3: Fund Deposit**
- Buyer deposits funds with Escrow Agent
- Escrow Agent confirms receipt
- All parties notified of successful deposit

### 5.2. Performance Phase

**Condition Fulfillment**:
- Seller performs obligations (warehouse prepared, access granted, etc.)
- Buyer confirms satisfactory condition
- Both parties communicate status to Escrow Agent

**Milestone Payments** (for phased releases):
- Funds released in stages as milestones are met
- Each release requires confirmation from Buyer
- Specific milestones defined in Exhibit A

### 5.3. Release Phase

**Normal Release**:
- All conditions met and confirmed
- Joint release instruction from Buyer and Seller, OR
- Unilateral release from Buyer confirming satisfaction
- Escrow Agent releases funds to Seller

**Contingency Release**:
- If conditions not met, funds returned to Buyer
- Platform mediates if there is disagreement
- Dispute resolution procedures followed if needed

## 6. Release Conditions

6.1. **Warehouse Storage Contracts**: Typical release conditions:
- Warehouse space available and accessible
- Space meets specifications in booking agreement
- Security measures and insurance confirmed
- Access control systems operational
- Initial inspection completed and approved by Buyer

6.2. **Service Completion**: For service-based releases:
- Services performed as specified
- Quality standards met
- Documentation provided (if required)
- Buyer confirmation received

6.3. **Phased Releases**: For long-term contracts:
- **Initial Release (30%)**: Upon space availability and initial inspection
- **Monthly Releases (60% total)**: Upon satisfactory monthly service
- **Final Release (10%)**: Upon contract completion and final inspection

6.4. **Time-Based**: Automatic release after specified period if no objections raised:
- Typically 7-14 days after service commencement
- Buyer must raise concerns within the inspection period

## 7. Inspection and Acceptance

7.1. **Inspection Period**: 
- Buyer has 7 business days from space availability for initial inspection
- Extended inspection period (up to 30 days) for large or complex warehouses

7.2. **Acceptance Criteria**:
- Space dimensions and specifications match booking
- Safety and security systems operational
- Cleanliness and condition acceptable
- Access and logistics as represented
- All required permits and insurance in place

7.3. **Rejection**: If Buyer rejects:
- Must provide specific reasons in writing
- Seller has opportunity to remedy deficiencies (typically 15 days)
- If remedy unsuccessful, funds returned to Buyer minus any agreed partial payments

7.4. **Deemed Acceptance**: 
- If Buyer takes possession and begins using space, this constitutes acceptance
- If Buyer does not object within inspection period, this may constitute deemed acceptance

## 8. Escrow Fees

8.1. **Fee Structure**:
- Standard escrow fee: 1.5% of escrow amount
- Minimum fee: 1,000 TRY
- Maximum fee: 25,000 TRY
- International transactions: Additional 0.5% for currency conversion and compliance

8.2. **Fee Allocation**:
- By default: Buyer and Seller split fees 50/50
- Parties may agree to alternative allocation
- Platform may subsidize fees for promotional purposes

8.3. **Additional Fees**:
- Dispute resolution: 500 TRY per hour (if extended mediation required)
- Legal review (if requested): Separate fee as quoted
- Amendment to escrow instructions: 250 TRY per amendment
- Extended hold period (beyond 90 days): 0.1% per month

8.4. **Fee Payment**: 
- Fees typically deducted from escrow amount at release
- May be paid separately by agreement

## 9. Amendments to Escrow Instructions

9.1. **Mutual Agreement**: Escrow instructions may be amended by written agreement of all parties.

9.2. **Amendment Process**:
- Amendment request submitted in writing
- All parties review and approve
- Escrow Agent confirms feasibility
- Amendment fee applies (250 TRY)

9.3. **No Unilateral Changes**: No party may unilaterally change escrow instructions.

## 10. Dispute Resolution

10.1. **Escalation Path**:

**Level 1: Direct Negotiation** (Days 1-7)
- Parties attempt to resolve directly
- Platform provides mediation support

**Level 2: Platform Mediation** (Days 8-21)
- Platform formally mediates
- Review of evidence and documentation
- Non-binding recommendation issued

**Level 3: Formal Arbitration** (Days 22+)
- Binding arbitration if mediation fails
- Arbitrator appointed per Platform Terms of Service
- Decision is final and binding

10.2. **Hold During Disputes**:
- Funds remain in escrow during dispute resolution
- Escrow Agent continues to hold until resolution
- Extended hold fees may apply after 90 days

10.3. **Evidence**:
- Photos and documentation of warehouse condition
- Communication records between parties
- Inspection reports
- Expert assessments (if obtained)

10.4. **Costs**: Dispute resolution costs typically borne by non-prevailing party, or split per arbitrator's decision.

## 11. Cancellation and Refunds

11.1. **Pre-Service Cancellation**: 
- If cancelled before service commencement, funds returned to Buyer
- Cancellation fees per Booking Agreement apply
- Escrow fees non-refundable

11.2. **Post-Service Cancellation**:
- Prorated payments based on service rendered
- Seller receives payment for completed period
- Remaining funds returned to Buyer (minus any applicable penalties)

11.3. **Force Majeure Cancellation**:
- If service cannot be provided due to force majeure, full refund to Buyer
- Escrow fees may be waived in extraordinary circumstances

## 12. Seller Protection

12.1. **Payment Assurance**: Escrow assures Seller that funds are available and committed.

12.2. **Automatic Release**: If Buyer does not object within inspection period, funds automatically released per escrow instructions.

12.3. **Bad Faith Claims**: Protection against frivolous rejection claims by Buyer.

12.4. **Performance Incentive**: Partial releases reward Seller for meeting milestones.

## 13. Buyer Protection

13.1. **Service Guarantee**: Funds not released until Seller performs obligations.

13.2. **Quality Assurance**: Inspection period ensures space meets expectations.

13.3. **Refund Security**: If service not provided, full refund guaranteed.

13.4. **Dispute Rights**: Right to halt payment if legitimate concerns about service quality.

## 14. Platform's Role

14.1. **Facilitation**: Platform facilitates escrow arrangement but is not a party to the escrow.

14.2. **Mediation**: Platform provides mediation services for disputes.

14.3. **Communication**: Platform coordinates communication between parties and Escrow Agent.

14.4. **No Liability**: Platform is not liable for Escrow Agent's performance or for disputes between parties.

14.5. **Fee Subsidies**: Platform may subsidize escrow fees for qualifying transactions.

## 15. Escrow Agent's Role and Duties

15.1. **Duties**:
- Hold funds securely
- Follow escrow instructions precisely
- Remain neutral and impartial
- Maintain confidentiality
- Provide regular status updates
- Release funds only per instructions or legal order

15.2. **No Advisory Role**: Escrow Agent does not provide legal or business advice.

15.3. **Liability**: Escrow Agent liable only for gross negligence or willful misconduct.

15.4. **Interpleader**: If conflicting claims, Escrow Agent may file interpleader action and deposit funds with court.

## 16. Legal and Regulatory Compliance

16.1. **Anti-Money Laundering (AML)**: 
- Escrow Agent performs KYC/AML checks
- Suspicious transactions reported to authorities
- Parties must provide identification and documentation

16.2. **Tax Reporting**: 
- Escrow releases may be reported to tax authorities
- Parties responsible for their own tax obligations

16.3. **Cross-Border Compliance**: International escrows subject to additional regulations:
- Foreign exchange controls
- International sanctions screening
- Additional documentation requirements

## 17. Termination of Escrow

17.1. **Normal Termination**: Escrow terminates upon:
- Completion of all release conditions
- Full payment to Seller or refund to Buyer
- Final accounting and documentation

17.2. **Early Termination**: By mutual written agreement of all parties.

17.3. **Legal Termination**: By court order or legal requirement.

17.4. **Final Documentation**: Escrow Agent provides final statement and releases all parties.

## 18. Confidentiality

18.1. **Confidential Information**: Escrow arrangement and all related documentation are confidential.

18.2. **Permitted Disclosures**: 
- To legal or financial advisors
- As required by law or regulation
- To Platform for dispute resolution

18.3. **Escrow Agent**: Bound by strict confidentiality obligations.

## 19. General Provisions

19.1. **Governing Law**: Turkish law governs this Agreement.

19.2. **Entire Agreement**: This Agreement and Exhibit A constitute the entire escrow agreement.

19.3. **Amendments**: Must be in writing and signed by all parties.

19.4. **Severability**: Invalid provisions do not affect remaining provisions.

19.5. **Notices**: All notices must be in writing to addresses on file.

19.6. **Assignment**: No party may assign rights or obligations without consent.

19.7. **Counterparts**: May be executed in counterparts.
