# Dispute Resolution Policy

**Effective Date:** January 10, 2026  
**Version:** 1.0

## 1. Policy Overview

This Dispute Resolution Policy ("Policy") establishes procedures for resolving disputes between users of TSmart Warehouse ("Platform") and between users and the Platform. We are committed to resolving disputes fairly, efficiently, and amicably.

## 2. Scope of Coverage

2.1. **Covered Disputes**: This Policy applies to:
- Disputes between Customers and Warehouse Owners
- Disputes between Partners (Resellers, Finders) and Platform
- Disputes between users and Platform regarding services
- Billing and payment disputes
- Service quality disputes
- Data or privacy disputes
- Breach of contract claims
- Intellectual property disputes related to Platform use

2.2. **Excluded Disputes**: This Policy does NOT apply to:
- Criminal matters (report to authorities)
- Disputes with third parties not using the Platform
- Personal matters unrelated to Platform services
- Disputes already in litigation when this Policy takes effect

## 3. Resolution Hierarchy

We follow a tiered approach to dispute resolution:

```
Level 1: Direct Communication (1-7 days)
         ↓ (if unresolved)
Level 2: Platform Mediation (8-21 days)
         ↓ (if unresolved)
Level 3: Formal Arbitration (22+ days)
         ↓ (final)
Legal Action (last resort, limited circumstances)
```

## 4. Level 1: Direct Communication

4.1. **Initial Step**: Parties must first attempt to resolve disputes directly:
- Communicate clearly and professionally
- Use Platform messaging system (creates record)
- Clearly state the issue and desired resolution
- Respond within 48 hours
- Negotiate in good faith

4.2. **Documentation**: Keep records of:
- All communications
- Supporting evidence (photos, receipts, contracts)
- Timeline of events
- Attempts at resolution

4.3. **Duration**: Allow 7 days for direct resolution before escalating.

4.4. **Resolution**: If resolved:
- Document agreement in writing
- Both parties confirm resolution through Platform
- Platform may request confirmation for record-keeping

## 5. Level 2: Platform Mediation

5.1. **Escalation**: If direct communication fails, either party may request Platform mediation:
- Submit request via: disputes@tsmart.com or through dashboard
- Include: Account details, other party info, issue description, desired outcome, supporting evidence
- Response: Platform acknowledges within 24 hours

5.2. **Mediation Process**:

**Day 1-3: Review and Assessment**
- Dispute coordinator assigned
- Both parties contacted
- Evidence and documents reviewed
- Issue and facts clarified

**Day 4-14: Mediation Sessions**
- Coordinator facilitates communication
- May conduct individual discussions with each party
- Explore potential resolutions
- Identify common ground
- Propose compromise solutions

**Day 15-21: Resolution Proposal**
- Coordinator proposes fair resolution
- Based on: Platform policies, evidence, industry standards, fairness
- Proposal sent to both parties
- Parties have 5 days to accept or reject

5.3. **Mediation Outcomes**:

**Accepted Resolution**:
- Agreement documented in writing
- Both parties sign acknowledgment
- Platform may implement resolution (refunds, etc.)
- Case closed

**Rejected Resolution**:
- Coordinator may propose revised resolution
- If still rejected, escalates to Level 3 (Arbitration)

5.4. **Fees**: Platform mediation is free for users.

5.5. **Mediator Role**:
- Facilitates communication
- Proposes solutions
- Does NOT make binding decisions (unless parties agree)
- Maintains neutrality

## 6. Level 3: Binding Arbitration

6.1. **When Required**: If Levels 1 and 2 fail to resolve dispute.

6.2. **Arbitration Agreement**: By using Platform, you agree to binding arbitration for unresolved disputes.

6.3. **Arbitration Rules**: Conducted under:
- Istanbul Arbitration Centre (ISTAC) rules (for Turkish parties)
- International Chamber of Commerce (ICC) rules (for international disputes)
- Or other mutually agreed arbitration body

6.4. **Arbitrator Selection**:
- Single arbitrator for disputes under 100,000 TRY
- Panel of 3 arbitrators for disputes over 100,000 TRY
- Selected from approved list or per arbitration rules
- Must be neutral and independent

6.5. **Arbitration Process**:

**Initiation**:
- Filing party submits arbitration request
- Includes: Statement of claim, evidence, desired remedy
- Copy to other party and Platform

**Response**:
- Other party responds within 30 days
- Includes: Statement of defense, counterclaims (if any), evidence

**Discovery**:
- Limited document exchange
- No depositions (unless arbitrator allows)
- Focus on efficiency

**Hearing**:
- In-person or virtual hearing
- Each party presents case
- Witnesses may testify
- Typically completed in 1-2 days

**Decision**:
- Arbitrator issues written award within 30 days of hearing
- Award is final and binding
- Enforceable in court

6.6. **Arbitration Location**:
- Istanbul, Turkey (for Turkish parties)
- Mutually agreed location (for international disputes)
- Virtual arbitration available

6.7. **Language**: 
- Turkish for domestic disputes
- English for international disputes
- Simultaneous translation provided if needed

6.8. **Costs**: 
- Each party pays own attorneys' fees
- Arbitration fees split 50/50 unless arbitrator decides otherwise
- Prevailing party may be awarded costs at arbitrator's discretion

6.9. **Exceptions to Arbitration**:
- Small claims court (for claims under small claims limit)
- Injunctive relief (to prevent immediate harm)
- Intellectual property disputes (may be litigated)

## 7. Specific Dispute Types

### 7.1 Booking Disputes

**Common Issues**:
- Warehouse not as described
- Availability or access problems
- Damage to stored goods
- Early termination disputes

**Resolution Approach**:
- Review booking agreement and warehouse listing
- Compare actual conditions to representations
- Consider photographic evidence
- Assess responsibility and fault
- Determine appropriate compensation

**Possible Remedies**:
- Partial or full refund
- Credit for future booking
- Payment for damages
- Contract termination without penalty

### 7.2 Payment Disputes

**Common Issues**:
- Disputed charges
- Refund requests
- Payment failures
- Pricing discrepancies

**Resolution Approach**:
- Review payment records and receipts
- Verify authorized charges
- Check for system errors
- Review applicable policies (cancellation, refund)

**Possible Remedies**:
- Refund to original payment method
- Platform credits
- Payment plan adjustments
- Correction of billing errors

### 7.3 Service Quality Disputes

**Common Issues**:
- Inadequate security
- Facility maintenance issues
- Access or communication problems
- Breach of service standards

**Resolution Approach**:
- Review service agreement and SLA
- Gather evidence of service failures
- Assess impact on customer
- Determine if breach is material

**Possible Remedies**:
- Service credits or discounts
- Facility improvements required
- Contract modification
- Termination with refund

### 7.4 Review and Rating Disputes

**Common Issues**:
- Fake or fraudulent reviews
- Defamatory content
- Review removal requests
- Rating manipulation

**Resolution Approach**:
- Verify reviewer's actual experience
- Check for policy violations
- Assess factual accuracy
- Consider intent and context

**Possible Remedies**:
- Review removal or editing
- Response opportunity for business
- Account suspension for fraud
- Corrective statement

### 7.5 Data and Privacy Disputes

**Common Issues**:
- Unauthorized data use
- Privacy violations
- Data breach complaints
- Data subject rights requests

**Resolution Approach**:
- Review data processing agreements
- Verify compliance with KVKK/GDPR
- Assess harm and damages
- Consider regulatory requirements

**Possible Remedies**:
- Data deletion or correction
- Enhanced privacy controls
- Compensation for breach
- Improved security measures

## 8. Evidence and Documentation

8.1. **Types of Evidence**:
- Written communications (emails, messages)
- Photos and videos
- Contracts and agreements
- Receipts and payment records
- Screenshots
- Witness statements
- Expert reports (if applicable)

8.2. **Evidence Standards**:
- Must be relevant to dispute
- Must be authentic (not altered)
- Must be timely (related to dispute period)
- Hearsay may be considered but given less weight

8.3. **Platform Access**: Platform may provide:
- Communication records
- Transaction history
- Account activity logs
- Warehouse listing snapshots
- (With appropriate privacy protections)

## 9. Timelines and Deadlines

9.1. **Response Times**:
- Initial escalation response: 24 hours
- Response to mediation invitation: 5 days
- Response to proposed resolution: 5 days
- Response to arbitration filing: 30 days

9.2. **Overall Timelines**:
- Direct communication: 7 days
- Platform mediation: 14-21 days
- Arbitration: 60-120 days (varies)

9.3. **Extensions**: May be granted for:
- Good cause shown
- Mutual agreement of parties
- Complexity of dispute
- Availability of arbitrator

9.4. **Failure to Respond**:
- Default judgment may be entered
- Unfavorable presumptions
- Costs assessed against non-responding party

## 10. Confidentiality

10.1. **Confidential Process**: Dispute resolution is confidential:
- Communications not public
- Evidence not disclosed outside process
- Settlement terms confidential (unless public filing required)

10.2. **Exceptions**:
- As required by law or court order
- To enforce settlement or arbitration award
- With consent of all parties
- Anonymized data for improving processes

10.3. **Non-Disparagement**: Parties agree not to publicly disparage each other during and after resolution (except for factual, non-defamatory statements).

## 11. Remedies and Enforcement

11.1. **Available Remedies**:
- **Monetary**: Refunds, damages, compensation
- **Specific Performance**: Require party to fulfill obligation
- **Injunctive Relief**: Stop harmful conduct
- **Declaratory Relief**: Clarify rights and obligations
- **Account Actions**: Suspension, termination, restrictions

11.2. **Limitations**:
- No punitive damages (except as required by law)
- Damages limited to actual proven losses
- No damages for emotional distress (except in exceptional cases)
- Platform liability limited per Terms of Service

11.3. **Enforcement**: 
- Platform facilitates agreed remedies
- Arbitration awards enforceable in court
- Legal action for non-compliance with binding decision

## 12. Appeals and Modification

12.1. **Arbitration Awards**: Generally final with very limited appeal rights:
- Fraud or corruption
- Arbitrator exceeded authority
- Fundamental procedural unfairness
- Award violates public policy

12.2. **Mediated Agreements**: May be modified by mutual written agreement.

12.3. **Correction of Errors**: Obvious errors (typos, calculation mistakes) may be corrected without appeal.

## 13. Class Actions and Collective Disputes

13.1. **Class Action Waiver**: By using Platform, you agree:
- Disputes resolved individually, not in class actions
- No consolidation with other disputes (except by mutual agreement)
- No representative actions on behalf of others

13.2. **Exception**: If class action waiver is unenforceable under applicable law, this arbitration provision does not apply.

13.3. **Opt-Out**: You may opt out of arbitration agreement:
- Send written notice within 30 days of account creation
- To: legal@tsmart.com
- Opt-out does not affect other Terms of Service

## 14. Emergency and Interim Relief

14.1. **Urgent Matters**: For issues requiring immediate action:
- Contact emergency hotline: [Phone]
- Request temporary relief from arbitrator
- Seek court injunction if necessary to prevent irreparable harm

14.2. **Types of Emergency Relief**:
- Temporary restraining orders
- Preliminary injunctions
- Asset preservation orders
- Expedited arbitration

14.3. **Standard**: Must show:
- Immediate and irreparable harm
- Likelihood of success on merits
- Balance of harms favors relief
- Public interest served

## 15. International Disputes

15.1. **Applicable Law**:
- Turkish law for disputes involving Turkish users
- Law of user's country for certain consumer disputes (as required by law)
- International conventions for cross-border commercial disputes

15.2. **Language and Translation**:
- Proceedings in Turkish or English
- Translation provided at party's expense
- Official language specified at outset

15.3. **Enforcement**: Arbitration awards enforceable under:
- New York Convention (international arbitration)
- Bilateral treaties
- Local enforcement procedures

## 16. Platform's Role

16.1. **Neutral Facilitator**: Platform serves as neutral facilitator, not advocate for either party.

16.2. **Platform as Party**: When Platform is a party to dispute:
- Independent mediator or arbitrator used
- Platform provides access to relevant data
- Same procedures apply

16.3. **Discretionary Actions**: Platform may take interim actions:
- Suspend accounts pending resolution
- Hold funds in escrow
- Restrict platform features
- To protect users or Platform integrity

## 17. User Responsibilities

17.1. **Good Faith**: Participate in good faith:
- Provide truthful information
- Respond promptly
- Consider reasonable compromises
- Respect process and other parties

17.2. **Compliance**: Comply with:
- Deadlines and procedures
- Discovery requests
- Arbitrator orders
- Final decisions

17.3. **Costs**: Pay your share of costs:
- Attorney fees (if you choose representation)
- Arbitration fees (your portion)
- Expert witness fees (if you use experts)

## 18. Modifications to Policy

18.1. **Updates**: Platform may update this Policy:
- To improve process efficiency
- To comply with legal requirements
- To clarify procedures

18.2. **Notice**: 30 days advance notice of material changes.

18.3. **Grandfathering**: Disputes arising before changes governed by Policy in effect at time dispute arose.

## 19. Governing Law and Venue

19.1. **Governing Law**: Turkish law governs this Policy and dispute resolution procedures.

19.2. **Judicial Venue**: For matters not subject to arbitration:
- Turkish users: Istanbul courts
- International users: As determined by conflict of laws principles

19.3. **Language**: Proceedings in Turkish for domestic disputes; English may be used for international disputes.
